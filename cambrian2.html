The apex predator of the Cambrian Era. The anomalocaris is an extinct aquatic arthropod with fossil evidence suggesting that it lived rougly 508 million years ago. This alien-looking creature is defined as one of the most iconic animals from Earth's cambrian era, serving as one of the first apex predators to ever have lived. Other notable features include its relatively large size compared to other creatures that roamed the cambrian seas during the time. They primarily fed on soft-bodied animals which included worms and other arthropods and some fossil evidence suggesting they may have fed on trilobites. One of the anomalocaris's most unique features are its massive eyestalks. These stalked eyes possessed a wide number of lenses, leading many to suggest the anomalocaris had excellent vision for hunting compared to any creature during its time. Surprisingly, the anomalocaris is not known to have evolved into any current living species, as it is an extinct genus of early arthropods. However, it is widely considered to be related to modern arthropods such as insects, crustaceans, and spiders.

One of the most alien-looking creatures within Earth's animal kingdom. The opabinia is yet another creature that dwelled within the Cambrian seas. Like every other animal that lived during the Cambrian Era, this creature sparks an appearance that many consider to be not from this world. However, unlike the anomalocaris, the opabinia possesses five eyes, which likely played a role in spotting predators and prey from various angles. Other creatures that define the distinct appearance of this creature is its proboscis shaped claw located on the front of its head. This proboscis shaped claw is widely considered to have been used for feeding and possibly grasp food or probe the seafloor for edible particles. They primarily fed on small inverdebrates using their proboscis claw. Having had lived approximately 505 million years ago during the middle of the Cambrian period, the opabinia is thought to be closely related to modern day insects and crustaceans just like the anomalocaris.

Literally a cross between a shrimp and a small whale. The hurdia is an exitnct genus of marine arthropod which lived during the Middle Cambrian epoch, approximately 505 million years ago. Fossils of this creature were found in various locations such as North America, China, and the Czech Republic. One of its most famous characteristics is its prominent head carapace or shield. This large three-part structure extended from the front of its head and occupised a significant portion of its total body length. This body feature's main purpose in not currently known but some have suggested it played a role in feeding or navigating the sea floor. The hurdia is also thought to be closely related to the anomalocaris, as both creatures belong to the radiodont order of arhtopods. The hurdia possessed large eyes, prominent appendages, and a circular mouth with teeth. These physcial traits propose that the hurdia was an active hunter. Fossil evidence suggests it may have used its frontal appendages to stir up sediment and trap smaller prey. The hurdia is also closely ressembles and is related to a similar looking creature called the aegirocassis, which lived during the Ordovician period after the Cambrian Epoch.

Something out of a nightmare or hallucination. The hallucigenia is an extinct panrthropod known for its bizarre worm-like appearance. Having lived approximately 508 million years ago, the hallucigenia served as an early member of the panarthropod lineage, which includes modern day velvet worms, water bears, amd arthropods. The creature possessed a worm-like body with a row of spines along its back and seven or eight paris of clawed legs. Although it does feature its very odd and alien-looking appearance like every other creature that lived during the Cambrian Era, the hallucigenia was only the size of a human finger, meaning it was quite small compared to some of the other creatures during its time. With its small size, it liekly fed by sucking up microscopic organisms. Its mouth, surrounded by teeth-like strcutrues, suggests a suction-feeding habit rather than chewing down their food. Some hallucigenia fossils were also found near sea sponge fossils, which suggests it might have used its clawed legs to cling to them and feed.
